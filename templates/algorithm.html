ALGORITHM

1. \( \text{OPT}(i,j) \) is the maximum number of base pairs in a secondary structure for \( b_i b_{(i+1)} \dots b_j \).

2. \( \text{OPT}(i,j) = 0 \) if \( i \geq j-4 \).

3. In the optimal secondary structure on \( b_i b_{(i+1)} \dots b_j \),

  • (i) If \( j \) is not a member of any pair, use \( \text{OPT}(i,j-1) \).

  • (ii) If \( j \) pairs with some \( t < j-4 \), the knot condition yields two independent subproblems: \( \text{OPT}(i,t-1) \) and \( \text{OPT}(t+1,j-1) \).

    • RECURRENCE RELATION

    MathJax Example
    \[ OPT(i,j) = \max \begin{cases} OPT(i,j - 1) & \text{if } j \text{ is not a member of any pair} \\ \max\limits_{t}(1 + OPT(i,t - 1) + OPT(t + 1, j - 1)) & \text{if } j \text{ pairs with some } t < j - 4 \end{cases} \]

    \( t \) ranges from \( i \) to \( j - 5 \), such that it is allowed to pair with \( j \)
