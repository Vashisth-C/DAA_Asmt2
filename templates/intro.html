INTRODUCTION

The RNA folding problem is a crucial challenge in bioinformatics, aiming to predict the three-dimensional structure of RNA molecules from their primary sequence. RNA molecules fold into intricate structures essential for their biological functions, such as protein synthesis, gene regulation, and catalysis.

Dynamic programming is a powerful algorithmic technique frequently employed to solve optimization problems by breaking them down into simpler subproblems. In the context of RNA folding, dynamic programming algorithms, like the Nussinov algorithm or the Zuker algorithm, use a recurrence relation to efficiently compute the optimal secondary structure of an RNA molecule.
