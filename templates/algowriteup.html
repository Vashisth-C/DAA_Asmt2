RNA FOLDING PROBLEM

WHAT IS RNA

RNA is a basic biological molecule. It is single-stranded. RNA molecules fold into complex secondary structure, often governing the behavior of an RNA molecule.

There are various rules that govern the formation of the secondary structure:

1. Pairs of the bases match up; each base matches with just one other base.

2. Adenine (A) always matches with Uracil (U), while Guanine (G) always matches Cytosine (C).

3. There are no kinks in the folded molecule.

4. Structures are knot-free.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Given an RNA molecule, we have to predict its secondary structure by implementing the Dynamic Programming Algorithm.

FORMULATION OF THE RNA FOLDING PROBLEM

An RNA molecule is a string, \( B = b_1b_2b_3...b_n \), where each \( b_i \) belongs to \(\text{\{A,U,G,C\}}\) .

A secondary structure on \( B \) is a set of pairs \( S = \{(i,j)\} \), where \( 1 \leq i, j \leq n \), and satisfies the following rules:

1. No Sharp Turns: The ends of each pair are separated by at least 4 intervening bases, i.e., if \( (i,j) \) belongs to \( S \), then \( i < j-4 \).

2. The elements in each pair in \( S \) consist of either \(\text{\{A,U\}}\) or \(\text{\{G,C\}}\) in either order.

3. \( S \) is a matching: No base appears in more than one pair.

4. No Knots: If \( (i,j) \) and \( (k,l) \) are two pairs in \( S \), then we cannot have \( i < j < k < l \).
