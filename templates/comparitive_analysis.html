Comparative Analysis

The comparative analysis of the pairing obtained from the RNA folding algorithm and the actual structures found in nature shows us that both the number of pairs and pairings are different.

The RNA folding algorithm finds the maximum possible number of pairings, but does not look into what each bond means in the grand scheme of things.

In nature, every bond and every pairing can lead to an incredible variety in functionality and structure of any organism as a whole.

Thus the issue with our code is that it can only look for the most optimal pairing in terms of counts of pairs, and not what those pairings would result in, yielding vastly different structures than those in real life.
